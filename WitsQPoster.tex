\begin{abstract}
Quantum Computing has grown in leaps and bounds in the last 5-10 years. Machine learning is a field where large data-sets and massively parallel computing architectures have allowed for similar rapid improvements. We propose the use of quantum computing for state space search based selection of initial weights or parameters for a machine learning model before training on classical architectures. This avenue of research may yield actionable results in the near future as opposed to waiting, the possibly many years, for quantum computers to have the ability to run effective machine learning algorithms. We propose that while the development of quantum machine learning techniques has been an incredible area of active research, quantum computing hardware has not yet reached and will probably not reach in the near future the capability to run anything more realistic than the simplest proof-of-concept type examples. Leveraging the exponential speedup of a quantum computer for state-space search to select either initial weights or model parameters combined with traditional training on data may yield better or faster learning while being feasible in the very near future.
\end{abstract}
